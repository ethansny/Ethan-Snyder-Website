The Problem

Video games have evolved significantly with advancements in technology and internet access.


Live Service games like Apex: Legends and Fortnight, which change over time, are gaining popularity.


Longtime players, having invested substantial time and money, expect clear communication with the game's creators and representatives.


Game developers currently use social media to communicate with players. However, these methods have drawbacks, with no protections for developers. How can two-way communication be promoted while safeguarding against harassment?

Competitive Analysis

To understand existing solutions that address this issue, I conducted a competitive analysis. Identified competitors include social media platforms like X (previously Twitter), Discord, email notifications, and custom game apps.

These options generally fall into two categories: overly restrictive, lacking safeguards, or insufficiently facilitating two-way communication.

Paper and Lo-Fi Prototypes

Creating paper and low-fidelity digital prototypes is crucial in our design process for rapid iteration. Paper prototypes, made with basic materials like paper and adhesive, allow us to quickly visualize and refine concepts.

Creating low-fidelity digital prototypes from paper prototypes, allow for user input on design flow without significant time investment.

Hi-Fi Prototype


The creation of a Hi Fidelity Prototype allows for users, developers, and stakeholds a more complete visualization of how the finished product will look and behave.

In the modern era, players expect communication from developers of games they love. Current methods use existing social media, but this has led to developers being harrassed

This platorm would consolidate social media communication, game functionality, and news into one loaction. Posting and commenting requires linking your account to your game profile to give methods to punish bad actors
